# Dungeon Design Ideas

## Landmarks

- Shrines: small, readable landmark rooms that can hold lore dressing, safe routes, optional loot, key pedestals, or puzzle clues.
- Collapsed halls: damaged corridor variants that break repetition, expose caves or hazards, create detours, and show unreachable rooms.
- Ritual chambers: larger set-piece rooms for minibosses, puzzles, boss keys, rare loot, or major theme moments.

## Required Progression

- Keep the large optional sprawl, but add guaranteed critical branches.
- Add a guaranteed boss-key route and a guaranteed boss-room route.
- Avoid fixed north/south repetition by using role-based pools such as `critical/key_path/early`, `critical/key_path/mid`, `critical/boss_path/locked_gate`, `critical/boss_key_room`, and `critical/boss_room`.
- Let the boss door be discoverable before the key so players have a clear objective.

## Room Gameplay

- Puzzle rooms should be Minecraft-native: switches, targets, timed hazards, traversal, sound/stealth, lava flow, or shulker levitation.
- Miniboss rooms can be encounter compositions rather than custom entities: spawner cages, arena hazards, elite mob groups, or themed traps.
- Micro-variations should be cheap overlays or alternate sockets: rubble, flooding, lava leaks, cobwebs, broken floors, hidden alcoves, clutter, and spawner nooks.

## Theme Gameplay

- Sculk: wool paths, sensors, shriekers, darkness, quiet traversal, and stealth pressure.
- Nether: lava gates, fire traps, blaze pressure, wither skeleton bridges, and fire-resistance rewards.
- End: shulker levitation traversal, floating platforms, chorus escape routes, dragon/end-crystal visual motifs, and vertical puzzles.
